For CMU-Q undergraduates

How to apply for an exchange semester.

Studying abroad sounds simple until you start, and then the information is scattered across offices, emails, and university sites. This guide puts the whole process in one place, in the order you actually do it.

Who it's for

Undergrads who want to study abroad and don't know where to begin

Start by

Two to three semesters before you want to go

What's inside

8 steps, 2 approval checkpoints, a document checklist

Start to finish

The eight steps

Follow these in order. Two of them are approval checkpoints, marked in red, and you need both before you can apply to the host university.

1

Research partner universities and check exchange agreements

Find which universities CMU-Q has exchange agreements with, then narrow to the ones that offer courses in your major. Build a shortlist before anything else.

Where to look: CMU-Q Student Affairs, your academic advisor, and the official CMU-Q study-abroad page. [Add the link here once you have it.]

2

Collect syllabi for the courses you want to take

For every course you might take abroad, get the host university's syllabus or course description. You will need these for course approval, so gather a few more than you need in case some are not approved or not offered.

Output: a folder of host-course syllabi and a draft list of intended courses.

3

Approval checkpoint 1 of 2 · do this first

Advisor approval

Get course approval from your academic advisor

Your advisor checks that the host courses map onto your CMU-Q requirements and will transfer for credit. This is the first of two separate approvals, and it comes before the Dean's.

Bring: your syllabi and intended course list. Output: your advisor's sign-off on the courses.

4

Approval checkpoint 2 of 2 · after your advisor signs off

Dean of Education approval

Get approval from the Dean of Education

This is a separate, second approval. The Dean of Education gives the final academic clearance for you to study abroad for the semester. You generally cannot move to the CMU-Q application without both this and your advisor's sign-off.

Confirm the exact form and process with the Dean of Education's office, since this is the step students most often get wrong.

5

Complete CMU-Q's internal exchange application

With both approvals in hand, submit CMU-Q's own exchange application and your approved course list. Watch the internal deadline closely, it is usually earlier than the host's.

Where: CMU-Q Student Affairs or the Dean of Education's office.

6

Apply directly to the host university

Once CMU-Q nominates and clears you, apply to the host as a visiting or exchange student. Each host has its own application, deadlines, and required documents.

Output: an acceptance letter from the host. You need this letter for your visa, so keep it safe.

7

Apply for a student visa

Start only after you have the host's acceptance. Requirements depend entirely on the host country, so check the embassy or consulate of that country for the current rules. Common items include your acceptance letter, passport, proof of funds, and photos.

Give yourself plenty of time. Visa processing can be slow, and it is the part most likely to delay your departure.

8

Arrange housing, travel, and health insurance

Apply for housing early, through the host university or privately. Book travel once your dates are firm. Get health insurance that is valid in the host country, and check what both CMU-Q and the host require.

Output: a confirmed place to live, a travel plan, and valid insurance before you leave.

No surprises

Who handles what

It is easy to assume someone else is taking care of a step. Here is who owns which part of the process.

CMU-Q

Advisor, Dean of Education, Student Affairs

  • Course approval and academic clearance
  • The internal exchange application
  • Nominating you to the host university
  • Confirming credit transfer

Host university

Your destination abroad

  • Their own application and acceptance
  • Course registration once you arrive
  • On-campus housing, where offered
  • Local orientation and student support

Embassy or consulate

Of the host country

  • Your student visa
  • The current entry requirements
  • Proof-of-funds and document rules

You

The applicant

  • Gathering documents and meeting deadlines
  • Booking travel
  • Arranging insurance
  • Confirming details with each office

Always confirm before you act. This guide brings the process together in one place, but deadlines, forms, and requirements change and vary by university and country. Check the current details with your academic advisor, the Dean of Education's office, CMU-Q Student Affairs, your host university, and the relevant embassy before relying on any single step.
